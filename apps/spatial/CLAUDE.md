# apps/spatial — proto AR-glasses companion

Spatial is not "AR/VR/WebXR generically." It is the **prototype of the AR-glasses companion** — the form factor that, on a 5–10 year horizon, replaces the phone the way the phone replaced the flip phone. Patterns developed here become the production patterns when the glasses ship. Discipline matches that stake.

See [`docs/doctrine/spatial-as-endgame.md`](../../docs/doctrine/spatial-as-endgame.md) for the thesis, the five spatial primitives, and the calm-AR rule that replaces the original "no panels" framing.

## Rules

1. **Five spatial primitives — creature, satellite, environment, attractor, presentation. Never disconnected window-manager panels.** `src/hud.ts` is the non-negotiable safety floor — read-only essentials (connection state, balance, active task). Everything else expresses live state through one of the five primitives: receipts as **satellites** orbiting the creature, other agents as **creatures** in the scene, memory as **environment**, goals as **attractors**, content surfaces (web pages, terminals, the slab) as **presentations** the motebit shows you — anchored to the creature's gesture, receding when work ends. Records (credentials, settled history, balances) live in summonable HUD overlays — see [`docs/doctrine/records-vs-acts.md`](../../docs/doctrine/records-vs-acts.md). The full thesis: [`docs/doctrine/spatial-as-endgame.md`](../../docs/doctrine/spatial-as-endgame.md).
2. **Doctrine is compile-time.** The `SpatialExpression = Satellite | Creature | Environment | Attractor` union + `registerSpatialDataModule<K>()` live in `@motebit/render-engine` (shared with every surface that renders a creature). Spatial keeps the doctrinal enforcement: widening the union to include arbitrary 2D-rectangle kinds (`"panel"` / `"list"` / `"card"`) is a `tsc` error, locked by `@ts-expect-error` assertions in `__tests__/spatial-expression.neg.test.ts`. The fifth primitive (`Presentation`) joins the union when its first consumer arrives — a typed extension, not a category break. Scene primitives live in the package; the calm-AR rule lives here.
3. **Panel parity with desktop/web/mobile is an anti-goal — but presentation parity is fine.** A balance dashboard cargo-culted from desktop is the wrong expectation to satisfy. A signed receipt the motebit shows you on the desktop slab AND presents to you on glasses is the right one. The split: dashboards (records) live in summonable HUD overlays; presentations (acts) live in the motebit's gesture. The medium-native question — "what does this look like when there's no phone or laptop" — is the right one. See `vision_spatial_canvas.md`, `vision_endgame_interface.md`, `vision_interactive_artifacts.md`: when they say "the creature is the last interface," they mean spatial should stop imitating the web surface, not abandon presentation.
4. **Receipts are the first shipping spatial expression.** `ReceiptSatelliteCoordinator` mounts an outer ring around the creature; each signed delegation becomes an orb whose hue tracks chain-verification state (amber → green / orange / red). Receipts are acts — signed events the motebit just performed — so orbiting matches the metaphor. Credentials, by contrast, are records and live in panels; the `CredentialSatelliteRenderer` primitive is preserved in `@motebit/render-engine` for ephemeral use only (a credential briefly orbits during the delegation that uses it, then fades). See [`docs/doctrine/records-vs-acts.md`](../../docs/doctrine/records-vs-acts.md).
5. **When in doubt, add a spatial expression, not a panel.** If you can't express a concept as a satellite, creature, environment, attractor, or presentation, that is a signal the concept belongs in configuration (settings) rather than the spatial surface. Defaults that make a motebit read as a daily companion rather than an app: body-anchored (not viewport-anchored), gaze-aware, voice-first, ambient when idle, gestures-on-objects (not menus), recedes on work-end. Departing from these defaults needs explicit justification — see [`docs/doctrine/spatial-as-endgame.md`](../../docs/doctrine/spatial-as-endgame.md) §"Default companion shape."
